# DailyStars Gamification Outline

## Purpose
This guide defines how to use Points, Stars, and Streaks together to keep kids engaged while building consistent homeschool and home-routine habits.

## Core Roles of Each System
- Points: Immediate reward for daily task completion.
- Stars: Long-term progression and status.
- Streaks: Consistency and momentum over time.

The systems should not overlap too much. Each one should motivate a different behavior.

## 1. Points (Immediate Reward)

### What Points Are
Points are earned each time a kid completes a task. They are short-term and can be spent.

### Why Points Matter
- Gives immediate positive feedback.
- Makes daily effort feel visible and rewarding.
- Supports a reward-shop economy controlled by parents.

### Recommended Point Scale
- Small task: 5 points
- Medium task: 10 points
- Big task: 20 points

Keep point values predictable so kids can plan and set goals.

### How to Use Points Well
- Show points earned today and total available points.
- Allow kids to spend points on parent-approved rewards.
- Avoid random point spikes that break trust in the system.

### Suggested Reward Shop Examples
- 30 points: Pick dessert
- 50 points: 20 minutes extra play
- 80 points: Choose family game
- 120 points: Pick a weekend activity

## 2. Stars (Long-Term Progression)

### What Stars Are
Stars represent lifetime progress. They are earned from cumulative points and are never spent.

### Why Stars Matter
- Gives kids a sense of growth over weeks and months.
- Encourages persistence beyond daily wins.
- Supports milestone celebrations and unlocks.

### Recommended Conversion Rule
- Every 100 lifetime points = 1 star

### What Stars Can Unlock
- Avatar frames
- Badge titles (for example, "Mission Master")
- Parent-defined privileges

### UI Recommendations for Stars
- Show current stars.
- Show progress to next star.
- Celebrate each new star with a small animation/message.

## 3. Streaks (Consistency Momentum)

### What Streaks Are
Streaks track consecutive successful routine days.

### Why Streaks Matter
- Reinforces follow-through and routine consistency.
- Encourages kids to keep momentum.
- Helps build habits over time.

### Recommended Streak Rule
A streak day counts only when all scheduled tasks for that day are completed.

### Streak Protection Ideas
- One streak shield per week to absorb a single off day.
- Skip days with no assigned tasks so streaks stay fair.

### Suggested Streak Bonuses
- Day 3+: +10% point bonus
- Day 7: streak chest / bonus reward
- Day 14+: bigger celebration

## Recommended Combined Economy

### Daily Loop
- Kid completes tasks -> earns points.
- If all tasks are completed for the day -> streak increases.
- Lifetime points accumulate -> stars increase over time.

### Weekly Loop
- Parent reviews point spending and streak health.
- Kids target one streak milestone and one star milestone.

### Monthly Loop
- Celebrate star growth and habit consistency.
- Adjust task difficulty and rewards based on engagement.

## Guardrails (Important)
- Do not make rewards too expensive at the beginning.
- Do not make all motivation dependent on one metric.
- Keep language positive and encouraging.
- Use clear goals: "2 tasks left for streak day" and "20 points to next star."

## Suggested Next Features for DailyStars
1. Parent-configurable reward shop tied to points.
2. Star milestone unlock messages and profile badges.
3. Streak shield with simple weekly reset logic.
4. Kid profile card showing:
   - Points today
   - Current stars
   - Current streak
   - Next milestone for each

## Summary
Use Points for immediate motivation, Stars for long-term pride, and Streaks for consistency. Together, these three systems create a balanced gamification model that helps homeschool families build steady routines without overwhelm.
